people of Austria

The people of Austria are called Austrians, and they are citizens of Austria. The term "Austrians" has been used since the 17th or 18th century. Culture Austria is known for its classical music, folk music, baroque architecture, coffee culture, winter sports, and Alpine traditions. Austria is a historically Catholic country. The official language of Austria is High German, but each region has its own dialect. Other languages spoken in Austria include Croatian, Slovenian, Hungarian, Turkish, and Serbian. Religion Christianity, particularly Roman Catholicism, is the predominant religion in Austria. In 2021, just over half (55.2%) of the population identified as Roman Catholic. Ethnicity 91% of the total population of Austria identify as ethnically Austrian. Other facts Austria has Europe's tallest waterfall. Europe's second longest river is in Austria. The Alps in Austria are a paradise for skiers and climbers. The Austrian flag is the second oldest in the world.
